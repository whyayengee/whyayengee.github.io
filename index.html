Semantic Open Source Software (SEMOSS)

Summer - Fall 2016

SEMOSS is a powerful visualization and analytics tool that has been rapidly evolving to deliver swift performance and insights for large data sets - Allowing the user to connect data from disparate data sources, run analytic algorithms, and create interactive visualizations to uncover insights in a matter of seconds. It started as a desktop application and has since evolved into a web application. I joined the project as a data analyst focusing on querying visualizations and constructing databases, and eventually shifted to re-designing the landing page and overall look & feel of the UI. Given its growing realm of applications and user base, the powerful analytics engine was due for a much-needed front-end facelift.

Given much creative freedom as the designer, I conducted research into similar applications and conducted user studies to work towards a more intuitive UI. In addition to incorporating current capabilities of the tool, I also had to prioritize a list of new & planned capabilities that were on the list to meet business needs. Below you can see snapshots of this design process.

An important capability of SEMOSS is the ability to create custom dashboards, and I also had the opportunity to design interactive dashboards capturing data on health information technology.

An Infographic Journey in Fitness

Winter 2016

I was an avid member of the OrangeTheory fitness club for 2 months. Their classes offer a 60-minute circut workout in a combination of cardio and strength training, backed by the science of Excess Post-Exercise Oxygen Consumption (EPOC). Members can choose to wear a heart rate monitor throughout class, which encourages you to maintain a target zone that stimulates metabolism and increases energy. Every day after class, a summary statement of your workout is sent to your inbox, recording data on your average heart rate, time spent in each zone, 'splat points' earned, and calories burned. I compiled all my data over two months into visualizations to see if there were any interesting patterns.

This personal project combined two of my favorite things - Fitness and Data Visualization.

Booth

Fall 2013 - Spring 2014 | In collaboration with Asian Student Association (ASA) | Carnegie Mellon University Campus | PC: Christina Yoon

Winning First Place in the "Independent Organization" Booth awards category of CMU Carnival 2014, the Asian Student Assocation's "Charlie and the Chocolate Factory"-themed booth attracted a mass of visitors in the Pittsburgh community. Limited to a footprint of 18 x 18 feet, the booth directs visitors through a series of exhibitions filled with student-made installation art. Each space within the booth reflects a different aspect of the beloved "Chocolate Factory" theme, based on the 2005 film adaptation. I was part of a design committee of five engineering, architecture, and design students who planned and managed the entire project from ideation to execution over the course of five months.

As structural designer & construction chair for this project, I developed the architectural blueprint (in SketchUp) down to specified dimensions and within structural requirements. I also led the team in gathering materials, managing timeline, and executing the on-site build process.
